Sportradar pulled in revenue of £561.2 million (US$623.6 million) for 2021, an increase of 39 per cent year-over-year (YoY) for the sports data and solutions firm.

Switzerland-based Sportradar credited strong growth across all business segments for the increase as its stock price rose more than 13 per cent on Nasdaq by end of trading on 30th March.

Adjusted earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortisation (Ebitda) for the full year rose 33 per cent YoY to £102 million (US$113 million).

Both revenue and Ebitda exceeded the company’s initial outlook.

Profit for 2021 was £12.8 million (US$14.2 million), down from the £14.8 million (US$16.4 million) for the previous year.

For the fourth quarter, Sportradar’s revenue climbed 41 per cent YoY to £152.4 million (US$169.3 million). US revenue leapt 92 per cent to £23.2 million (US$25.8 million).
